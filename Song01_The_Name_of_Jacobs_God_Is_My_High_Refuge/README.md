This song of confident refuge echoes Psalm 20 — “May the name of the God of Jacob protect you” — invoking the God of the patriarchs as high tower and deliverer. It is a prayer for help in trouble, remembrance of offerings, and anointing of God’s right hand. In the eternal day, this refuge is perfected — we dwell in His presence, His name on our foreheads forever (Revelation 22:4).
